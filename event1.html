CARNIVALESQUE 3.0 - DANCE AND FROLIC

ABSTRACT

Hey there, dance enthusiasts! Get ready to groove and twirl at exhilarating cultural dance competition- “Dance and Frolic”! Organised by Amity Institute of Studies and Research under our three day Literature Festival Carnivalesque 3.0. Immerse yourself in a vibrant celebration of diverse cultures through the universal language of dance. Show off your moves, unleash your creativity, and captivate the audience with your rhythmic prowess. Join us for an experience filled with electrifying performances, friendly competition, and the chance to win exciting prizes. Don’t miss out on this opportunity to shine on the dance floor! So, lace up your dancing shoes and let the “Dance and Frolic” extravaganza begin!

1. Concept of the event

In this competition, participants are supposed to perform any style of dance, including regional, national, or international forms, as a group, while adhering to the rules of each respective style.

2. Eligibility

For the junior group, it is 13 to 17 years of age. For the senior group, it is 17+ years of age. A separate category for Amity students.

3. Rules and Regulations (for offline)

  • There must be a particular theme of Dance in relation to our rich culture. Participants can pick a global dance form (Indian or Western) and perform any dance form - Kathak, Salsa, Jazz, Kathakali, Bharat Natyam, Manipuri, Kuchipudi, Mohiniyattam, Odissi, Sattriya, Hip-Hop etc.
  • Participants can either perform solo or in Group as well. Minimum number of participants in group dance is 2 and maximum is 5.
  • Time limit is 3-5 minutes. Negative points for exceeding the time limit.
  • Performances should showcase the cultural traditions and heritage with authenticity and respect.
  • Appropriate attire must be worn so that it doesn’t hurt any sentiment related to culture, faith and set of beliefs, ensuring it respects the traditions and norms of that culture.
  • Attire must go with cultural form/style of the dance.
  • Music selection should be culturally appropriate and respectful, representing the style and traditions of the dance being performed.
  • The participants will be judged on the basis of their costume, visual presentation, expression and co-ordination along with effective audience engagement.
  • Points will be given on aspects such as Taal, Technique, Rhythm, Abhinaya or Expression, Attire, Footwork, and overall presentation.
  • The decision of the jury will be final, no further claims shall be entertained.
  • Not abiding by any of the above-mentioned rules may lead to disqualification.
