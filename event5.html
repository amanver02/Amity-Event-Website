CARNIVALESQUE 3.0 - BATTLE OF BOOKS (DEBATE)

ABSTRACT

Are you passionate about the power of debate and the art of persuasion? Here’s your chance to shine. Step into the arena of intellectual combat at our Battle of the Books event. Join fellow thinkers and challengers as you navigate through a battlefield of ideas, where every argument is a strategic move and every rebuttal a tactical defense. This is more than just a competition; it’s a journey into uncharted territories of thought and discourse. Sharpen your rhetorical skills, forge alliances, and embrace the exhilarating clash of minds. Dare to challenge, dare to defend, and emerge victorious as the champion.

1. Concept of the event

In this competition, participants will engage in discussions and present their arguments on a given topic. The winner will be determined by the strength of their arguments.

2. Eligibility

For the junior group, it is 13 to 17 years of age. For the senior group, it is 17+ years of age. A separate category for Amity students.

3. Rules and Regulations (for offline)

  • The time limit for each participant is 4 minutes.
  • The medium of communication is English only.
  • Interrupting a speaker is forbidden.
  • Inappropriate usage of words or language is forbidden.
  • Time will be noted, and participants will be alerted with 10 seconds remaining.
  • The decision of the jury will stand final, no further clarifications will be entertained.
  • The use of mobile phone during the competition is strictly prohibited.

Topics:

Topic for Junior:

  • Are standardised exams effective in accurately evaluating student knowledge, skills, and academic achievement, considering factors such as test validity, reliability, and bias?
  • Should the role of teachers be replaced by AI and automated systems in education, and what are the implications for learning outcomes, student-teacher interactions, and the future of education?
  • Should the government ban violent video games, and what are the ethical, legal, and societal implications of such a policy on personal freedom and public health?

Topic for Senior:

  • Free and fair elections are essential for democracy, but are they actually being observed amid a lack of measures necessary to ensure their integrity and prevent manipulation?
  • Is there a need for increased government surveillance to enhance national security, balancing the potential benefits of preventing crime and terrorism with concerns regarding privacy rights, civil liberties, and the potential for abuse of power by authorities?
  • Is artificial intelligence a threat to job security, and should governments implement regulations to protect workers from potential job displacement?

Topics:

Topic for Junior:

  • Should schools prioritise the teaching of mathematics and science over art and humanities?
  • Should schools include religious studies as part of their curriculum, and what are the implications for promoting cultural understanding and religious tolerance in a diverse society?
  • Books are more informative and beneficial than digital media.

Topic for Senior:

  • Should social media platforms be subject to stricter censorship regulations to combat the spread of misinformation, hate speech, and harmful content, and while that is implemented, can freedom of expression be maintained?
  • Should governments allocate resources and prioritise investments in space exploration initiatives over addressing pressing issues and challenges in our society, such as poverty, healthcare, education, and climate change?
  • In today's rapidly evolving global marketplace, should businesses prioritize ethical considerations over profit maximization, or vice versa?
